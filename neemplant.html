Neem Plant (Azadirachta indica)

The Neem plant (Azadirachta indica) is a fast-growing evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent. It is widely recognized for its medicinal, agricultural, and environmental benefits.

Botanical Classification

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Order: Sapindales
  • Family: Meliaceae
  • Genus: Azadirachta
  • Species: A. indica

Health Benefits

Neem is known for its powerful medicinal properties. It is used in Ayurveda for its antibacterial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties.

  • Boosts immunity
  • Helps in skin care (treats acne, eczema, and other skin disorders)
  • Supports dental health (used in toothpaste and mouthwash)
  • Aids in digestion and liver health
  • Controls blood sugar levels

Agricultural & Environmental Benefits

Neem is a natural pesticide and is widely used in organic farming. Its leaves, bark, and oil act as insect repellents and fertilizers.

  • Used in biopesticides
  • Enhances soil fertility
  • Prevents desertification
  • Provides shade and improves air quality

Common Uses

Neem is used in various industries, including cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and agriculture.

  • Neem oil – Used in skincare and hair care products
  • Neem leaves – Used in traditional medicine and teas
  • Neem bark – Used in dental hygiene products
  • Neem-based pesticides – Used in organic farming
